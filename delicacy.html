Sate

Sate (or satay) is a popular Indonesian dish made of skewered and grilled meat, usually served with a rich, flavorful peanut sauce. The meat can be chicken, beef, lamb, or even seafood, and it's often marinated before grilling for extra taste. Sate is commonly enjoyed as street food and during special gatherings or festivals.

Rendang

Rendang is a spicy, rich Indonesian dish made from slow-cooked beef simmered in coconut milk and a blend of aromatic spices. Originating from the Minangkabau people of West Sumatra, it's known for its deep flavors and tender texture. Rendang is often served at festive occasions and is considered one of Indonesia's most iconic dishes.

Gado-Gado

Gado-Gado is a traditional Indonesian salad made with a mix of boiled vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and hard-boiled eggs, all topped with a creamy peanut sauce. It's a popular vegetarian dish that's both nutritious and flavorful. Often served with rice cakes or crackers, Gado-Gado is a staple in Indonesian cuisine.
